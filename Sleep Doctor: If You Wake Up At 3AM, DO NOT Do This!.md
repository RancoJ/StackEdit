### Core Concepts in Sleep

#### Sleep Systems in the Brain
- **Sleep Drive:** Builds like hunger through the accumulation of adenosine, making you feel sleepy.  
- **Sleep Rhythm (Circadian Rhythm):** Biological clock governed by melatonin and cortisol, determining the natural timing of sleep and wakefulness.

### Sleep Hygiene and Behavioral Tips

- **Wake up at the same time every day** to regulate melatonin production and circadian rhythm.  
- Avoid caffeine in the first 90 minutes after waking; hydrate first with 15-20 ounces of water to counteract dehydration from sleep and caffeine’s diuretic effect.  
- Alcohol before bed disrupts deep, restorative sleep (stage 3 and 4), critical for brain detoxification and reducing Alzheimer’s risk. If consuming alcohol, stop drinking 3 hours before bedtime and hydrate between drinks.

### Managing Middle-of-the-Night Awakenings

- Between 1-3 AM, the body naturally warms up and may cause brief awakenings.  
- Avoid getting out of bed unnecessarily (e.g., to pee) to keep heart rate low.  
- Use **478 breathing technique** (inhale 4 seconds, hold 7 seconds, exhale 8 seconds) to reduce heart rate and promote relaxation.  
- Avoid screens and clocks to prevent stimulation and anxiety.  
- If unable to sleep, practice **Non-Sleep Deep Rest (Yoga Nidra)** or get up to do light reading until sleepy.

### Sleep Disorders

#### Sleep Apnea
- Characterized by repeated airway obstruction during sleep causing snoring and breathing pauses.  
- Prevalence: 1 in 7 adults worldwide (~936 million people), with 80-90% undiagnosed.  
- Symptoms include loud snoring, gasping, morning headaches, mood swings.  
- Diagnosis via home sleep test measuring oxygen, heart rate, breathing events, and sleep stages.  
- Treatments:  
  - CPAP machines (air pressure masks) are most effective but sometimes uncomfortable.  
  - Oral appliances (jaw repositioners) and tongue vibration devices exist as alternatives.  
  - Surgical options and emerging drugs under development.  
- Women often underdiagnosed due to different symptom profiles, including frequent awakenings and headaches rather than loud snoring.

#### Insomnia
- Four types: difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, waking too early, and unrefreshing sleep.  
- Common causes: anxiety (~75%), depression, secondary to pain or other conditions.  
- Misconceptions: Trying to “catch up” on sleep by going to bed early worsens insomnia due to circadian misalignment.  
- Best treatment: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I), focusing on behavior modification and cognitive restructuring to reduce sleep anxiety.  
- Avoid excessive caffeine, alcohol, and overstimulation before bed.  
- Medication: Overprescription of sleep drugs is problematic; some OTC sleep aids (diphenhydramine) may increase Alzheimer’s risk.

- Dreams serve as **emotional metabolism**, helping process daytime emotions and trauma.

- Dream therapy involves journaling dreams, analyzing themes, and **changing dream endings** to reduce anxiety and improve sleep continuity.  
- Dreams are highly personal; symbolic interpretations without context are generally unhelpful.  
- Dream journaling and working with a therapist can aid in resolving recurrent nightmares.  
- Dreams may also serve as problem-solving and innovation tools for the brain.
